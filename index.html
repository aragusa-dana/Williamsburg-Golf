Williamsburg is FORE Golf Lovers!

The Williamsburg region boasts more than a dozen courses designed by some of the most notable architects, including Pete Dye, Rees Jones, Robert Trent Jones Sr., Arnold Palmer, Curtis Strange, John LeFoy and Nicklaus Design Associates. Noted for their scenic woodlands, rolling hills and challenging holes, these courses are recognized as some of the most playable in the country. If you haven't taken a good walk spoiled around one of the signature courses in the area, it's time to plan your trip. Dramatic renovations have transformed one course after another. The region has evolved as well with a unique collection of attractions before and after the 19th Hole including resorts, spas, award-winning restaurants and a tasting trail featuring beer, wine, and spirits all within a destination that is just 2.5 hours from Washington DC and Dulles International Airport. A rich legacy forms the foundation for the future of golf in the Williamsburg area, a future as one of the top golf destinations in the United States.


Explore Our Golf Resorts

A Williamsburg golf getaway offers 5-star luxury resorts or spacious condominiums with all the comforts of home.

Colonial Williamsburg
Resorts: Williamsburg Inn

Among the world's great hotels, the Williamsburg Inn offers unparalleled service, spectacular amenities and fine dining. Its proximity to the Revolutionary City, The Spa of Colonial Williamsburg and the Golden Horseshoe Golf Club make it a guest favorite.

Colonial Williamsburg
Resorts: Williamsburg Lodge

From a day spent at the spa to time on the golf course and everything in between, Williamsburg Lodge offers a relaxing retreat just steps away from all the action. As our guest you’ll also receive exclusive benefits such as admission ticket discounts and special daily events.

Colonial Williamsburg
Resorts: Gold Course

The legendary Robert Trent Jones, Sr. described the Gold Course as "one of his finest designs" upon completion in 1963. The highly-acclaimed, traditional layout received a multi-million dollar tune-up from Rees Jones in 2017 and the conditions are now better than ever.

Colonial Williamsburg
Resorts: Green Course

Rees Jones was tasked with complimenting his father's Gold Course design in 1991. The resulting Green Course is carved from the same beautiful parkland terrain, a little longer than the Gold but also more forgiving—creating an entertaining challenge for golfers of every skill level.

Colonial Williamsburg
Resorts: Spotswood Course

With six par-3's, two par-4's and one par-5, the Spotswood Course provides a great mix of shot values for the quick nine-hole experience. Although the length is great for beginners and juniors, the tiny greens will test the short game of all golfers.

Kingsmill Resort:
Accommodations

Kingsmill is a AAA Four‐Diamond resort located on the historic James River and is home of the annual LPGA Kingsmill Championship. The destination features guest rooms plus one-, two- and three-bedroom condominium accommodations.

Kingsmill Resort:
River Course

For more than three decades, The River Course has hosted the world’s best players on both PGA and LPGA tours. It has also hosted thousands of lesser-known golfing superstars—like you.

Kingsmill Resort:
Plantation Course

The Plantation Course is an Arnold Palmer and Ed Seay design that challenges players of all skill levels. The 6,432-yard, par-72 course favors accurate iron play and good putting.


Explore Our Courses

Spend a day on the course to experience top-rated golf by some of the most notable architects in the game.

Colonial Heritage Golf Club

Designed by world renowned Arthur Hills, Colonial Heritage is known for its superb playing surfaces and first-class hospitality.  With 6 different options for tees, the golf course measures from the back tees at 7,000 yards or from the most forward tees at 3,000 yards; providing an enjoyable option for any golfer.

Fords Colony Golf Club:
Blackheath

The most scenic of the three courses designed by Dan Maples. The course features bent grass greens and an island green on #18.

Fords Colony Golf Club:
Blue Heron

Another Dan Maples design that also features Champion Bermuda grass greens. Blue Heron offers the most challenging set of 18 greens on the property and winds through the Ford's Colony community.

Fords Colony Golf Club:
Marsh Hawk

The original course designed by Dan Maples at Ford's Colony with newly renovated Champion Bermuda grass greens. The nines return to the club house and feature rolling hills with large fairways and challenging greens.

Kiln Creek Golf Club

Designed by the highly respected architect Tom Clark, Kiln Creek Golf Club features a rolling terrain, generous driving areas, small greens, and numerous lakes and sandy wastes throughout the property. A premium is placed on approach shots as some putting surfaces can be particularly challenging to carelessly placed irons. 

Kiskiack Golf Club

Set along the ridge of a natural bluff, Kiskiack offers a day of golf for players of all levels. As our award-winning architect John LaFoy said "after playing Kiskiack, 18 golfers may each have a different favorite hole."

Williamsburg National
Golf Club: Jamestown Course

The Jamestown course design that truly captures that special blend of challenging, playable and fun. The greens were recently resurfaced with Champion Bermuda the same putting surface that is now found on many PGA Tour host site courses.

Williamsburg National
Golf Club: Yorktown Course

This popular Tom Clark design is WIlliamsburg's newest golf course. Modern Hybrid bent grass greens and 419 Bermuda fairways provide optimal playing conditions on a unique mix of holes, especially the back nine with three par 3's, three par 4's and three par 5's.
